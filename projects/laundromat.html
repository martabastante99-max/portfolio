Laundromat Market Study

A complete investment viability study for a self-service laundromat in Madrid — comparing three models, projecting 9 years of cash flows, and mapping every risk factor by zone.

The question

Is investing in a self-service laundromat in Madrid a viable opportunity — and if so, which model makes the most sense?

Three models evaluated

  • Business transfer (traspaso) — buying an existing operating laundromat
  • Franchise — partnering with an established brand
  • New build (obra nueva) — building from scratch in a new location

Analysis components

  • 9-year cash flow projections per model
  • Break-even analysis
  • Sensitivity analysis: electricity and water cost evolution (2020–2029)
  • Geographic risk mapping: market saturation, vandalism, energy dependency, franchise contract risk

Risk matrix highlights

Key risks identified: high energy cost volatility (especially post-2021), market saturation in central Madrid neighbourhoods, dependency on long-term franchise contracts, and vandalism exposure for unmanned locations. The new-build model showed the highest upside but also the longest payback period and highest execution risk.
