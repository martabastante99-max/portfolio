Background

My Story.

I studied Engineering Physics because I wanted to understand how things work at a fundamental level. I ended up applying that same instinct to markets, software, and businesses.

After my undergraduate degree I went straight into an MBA in Analytics, where I got serious about data, financial modelling and decision-making under uncertainty. Outside the classroom I was captain of the university tennis team for two years — which taught me more about leadership and managing pressure than most things I have read since. I was also part of the Investment Club and the Engineering Club, two places where I started connecting the dots between quantitative thinking and real-world decisions.

Since then I have worked across enterprise software, logistics, commodity trading and rail — always at the intersection of complex systems and the people trying to make sense of them.

Experience

Career.

Neurored — Salesforce-based Transport & CTRM Platform

Dec 2022 – Mar 2025 · Madrid
Business Analyst → Customer Success Manager

Neurored builds enterprise software for logistics and commodity trading on top of Salesforce. I joined while finishing my MBA, starting in a shadowing role and progressively taking on full project ownership across four continents.

Toyota Machinery Shadowing · Implementation

My entry point at Neurored. I shadowed the PM responsible for implementing Neurored's order management module for Toyota Machinery, a Japanese automotive parts division with operations in Kentucky, USA. This gave me a ground-level view of the full implementation lifecycle — from scoping workshops to go-live — and how to manage a client with very specific operational requirements across time zones.

Holcim Trading Application Maintenance · CTRM

Moved into application maintenance for Holcim, one of the world's largest building materials companies. The work centred on their commodity trading operations — managing the full trade lifecycle from opportunity to contract (both buy/sell and freight), invoices and payables, letters of credit, PDF document workflows, and mark-to-market valuations. This was my introduction to CTRM and the complexity of financial flows in physical commodity trading.

Cultura SA PM · 8-month Implementation

Took on my first full PM role for Cultura SA, a commodity trading company based in Malta operating primarily across North Africa. The implementation ran for around 8 months and covered their core trading workflows. After go-live, we built a budgeting and forecasting module for them — giving the finance team a live view of planned vs. actual performance across their portfolio.

XPD Global Lead CSM · €1.2M Account

Joined the XPD Global programme — Neurored's largest account, a €1.2M annual contract — working alongside two other PMs to improve and expand their freight forwarding system. Our team had 10 developers on the Neurored side and 2 on the client side. This is where my role started shifting toward product: I was involved in defining requirements, prioritising improvements, and aligning the roadmap with XPD's operational needs across LATAM, APAC and EMEA. During this period I also implemented the air cargo module for ProCarrier.

VarovaOy Workshops · Multimodal

Led discovery and scoping workshops for VarovaOy, a Finnish logistics company, designing a multimodal transport solution. All implementations follow the same rigorous process: pre-sale demos and training, structured workshops to close scope, project planning, and phased delivery with full client sign-off at each stage.

Commercial & Pre-Sales Ongoing

Alongside project work, I ran demos and discovery sessions for prospects including The Scoular Company and other international freight forwarders. I was also deeply involved in testing — deploying new versions, defining acceptance criteria, and coordinating QA between product and client teams.

Siemens Mobility — Sqills Revenue Management & Ticketing Platform

Apr 2026 – Jun 2026 · Madrid
Business Consultant

Sqills (a Siemens product) is a revenue management and ticketing platform for long-distance rail operators. In three months I was involved in three high-profile projects simultaneously, with a strong focus on partner demos and strategic market expansion.

Iryo Spain · Run Phase

Iryo was already live on the Sqills platform when I joined. My work focused on improvements and configuration enhancements to give their revenue managers more flexibility in the system — working closely with the technical and product teams to scope and deliver changes without disrupting live operations.

EFE Chile LATAM · Strategic Market Entry

A strategically critical project — EFE (Empresa de los Ferrocarriles del Estado) represents Sqills' first foothold in Latin America. Getting this right matters beyond the contract itself: it sets the foundation for the platform's expansion across the region.

Renfe Cercanías Spain · New Market Segment

Renfe's cercanías (commuter rail) network is a different operational model to long-distance rail — shorter journeys, higher frequency, different revenue logic. This project is about extending Sqills into that segment, opening a new market vertical for the platform in Spain.

Sonda Partner Demo · Mexico

Ran partner demos for Sonda, a major technology company in Latin America, as part of the commercial expansion strategy. Presenting a complex revenue management platform to a new market partner requires translating deep product knowledge into a compelling and accessible business case.
